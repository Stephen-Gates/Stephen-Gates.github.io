What is an open data certificate?

An open data certificate is an indicator of the quality of an open dataset. The criteria to earn a certificate are based on a number of global best practices that have been consolidated by the Open Data Institute.

Why earn a certificate for your open data?

  1. Re-users will be informed of:
    • What they can legally do.
    • Whether they can rely on the data.
    • What the data means.
    • Where to get some support.
  2. Publishers will be provided with:
    • A simple, semi-automated process to earn certificates.
    • A badge to display with their data to indicate its quality.
    • Automated suggestions to improve data quality.

To learn more about the open data certificate working group, email connect@odiqueensland.org.au.
